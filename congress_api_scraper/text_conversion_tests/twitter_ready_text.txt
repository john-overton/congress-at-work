𝐕𝐞𝐭𝐞𝐫𝐚𝐧𝐬 𝐁𝐞𝐧𝐞𝐟𝐢𝐭𝐬 𝐂𝐨𝐧𝐭𝐢𝐧𝐮𝐢𝐭𝐲 𝐚𝐧𝐝 𝐀𝐜𝐜𝐨𝐮𝐧𝐭𝐚𝐛𝐢𝐥𝐢𝐭𝐲 𝐒𝐮𝐩𝐩𝐥𝐞𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐚𝐥 𝐀𝐩𝐩𝐫𝐨𝐩𝐫𝐢𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧𝐬 𝐀𝐜𝐭, 𝟐𝟎𝟐𝟒:

𝐋𝐞𝐠𝐢𝐬𝐥𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐯𝐞 𝐉𝐨𝐮𝐫𝐧𝐞𝐲 𝐨𝐟 𝐇𝐑 𝟗𝟒𝟔𝟖

H.R. 9468, the 𝐕𝐞𝐭𝐞𝐫𝐚𝐧𝐬 𝐁𝐞𝐧𝐞𝐟𝐢𝐭𝐬 𝐂𝐨𝐧𝐭𝐢𝐧𝐮𝐢𝐭𝐲 𝐚𝐧𝐝 𝐀𝐜𝐜𝐨𝐮𝐧𝐭𝐚𝐛𝐢𝐥𝐢𝐭𝐲 𝐒𝐮𝐩𝐩𝐥𝐞𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐚𝐥 𝐀𝐩𝐩𝐫𝐨𝐩𝐫𝐢𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧𝐬 𝐀𝐜𝐭, 𝟐𝟎𝟐𝟒, commenced its legislative journey on September 6, 2024, when it was introduced in the House of Representatives. The bill was then referred to the House Committee on Appropriations.

On September 17, 2024, H.R. 9468 was the subject of debate in the House. Following forty minutes of discussion, Representative Mike Garcia moved to suspend the rules and pass the bill. The motion was agreed to by a voice vote. The House also agreed to lay the motion to reconsider on the table without objection. The bill then passed the House by a voice vote under a motion to suspend the rules.

The bill was received by the Senate on September 17, 2024. On September 19, 2024, the Senate passed the bill without amendment by a voice vote. Following the Senate's passage, a message regarding the Senate's action was sent to the House. On the same day, the bill was presented to the President, President Biden, for consideration.

On September 20, 2024, the President signed H.R. 9468 into law, officially becoming Public Law No: 118-82.

𝐁𝐢𝐥𝐥 𝐂𝐨𝐧𝐭𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐬

The 𝐕𝐞𝐭𝐞𝐫𝐚𝐧𝐬 𝐁𝐞𝐧𝐞𝐟𝐢𝐭𝐬 𝐂𝐨𝐧𝐭𝐢𝐧𝐮𝐢𝐭𝐲 𝐚𝐧𝐝 𝐀𝐜𝐜𝐨𝐮𝐧𝐭𝐚𝐛𝐢𝐥𝐢𝐭𝐲 𝐒𝐮𝐩𝐩𝐥𝐞𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐚𝐥 𝐀𝐩𝐩𝐫𝐨𝐩𝐫𝐢𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧𝐬 𝐀𝐜𝐭, 𝟐𝟎𝟐𝟒, primarily focuses on providing supplemental appropriations for the Department of Veterans Affairs for the fiscal year ending September 30, 2024.

𝐃𝐞𝐩𝐚𝐫𝐭𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭 𝐨𝐟 𝐕𝐞𝐭𝐞𝐫𝐚𝐧𝐬 𝐀𝐟𝐟𝐚𝐢𝐫𝐬 𝐀𝐩𝐩𝐫𝐨𝐩𝐫𝐢𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧𝐬

The bill allocates:

$2,285,513,000 for "Compensation and Pensions", to remain available until expended.
$596,969,000 for "Readjustment Benefits", to remain available until expended.

𝐆𝐞𝐧𝐞𝐫𝐚𝐥 𝐏𝐫𝐨𝐯𝐢𝐬𝐢𝐨𝐧𝐬

The bill includes several general provisions outlining how the appropriated funds should be managed. These include:

𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟏 states that the appropriations made available by this Act are in addition to any other amounts already appropriated for fiscal year 2024.
𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟐 specifies that, unless otherwise stated within the Act, the additional appropriations are subject to the same authorities and conditions as the existing appropriations for fiscal year 2024.

𝐑𝐞𝐩𝐨𝐫𝐭𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐑𝐞𝐪𝐮𝐢𝐫𝐞𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐬

The bill mandates the submission of reports from the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to the relevant Congressional committees:

𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟑(𝐚) requires a report detailing corrections the Department of Veterans Affairs will make to improve forecasting, data quality, and budget assumptions relating to budget submissions for funds under the headings "Compensation and Pensions" and "Readjustment Benefits". This report is to be submitted within 30 days of the Act's enactment.
𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟑(𝐛) necessitates a report on the status of funds appropriated under "Compensation and Pensions" and "Readjustment Benefits" for fiscal years 2024, 2025, and 2026. This report is to be submitted within 60 days of enactment and updated every 90 days until September 30, 2026.

𝐈𝐧𝐬𝐩𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐨𝐫 𝐆𝐞𝐧𝐞𝐫𝐚𝐥 𝐑𝐞𝐯𝐢𝐞𝐰

𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟒 mandates an Inspector General (IG) review of the circumstances surrounding the funding shortfalls within the Veterans Benefits Administration and the Veterans Health Administration.

Specifically, the IG is to examine:

𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟒(𝐚): The circumstances surrounding and underlying causes of the announced funding shortfall in the Veterans Benefits Administration for fiscal year 2024, as detailed in the Secretary of Veterans Affairs' July 19, 2024 letter to Congress, and the announced funding shortfall in the Veterans Health Administration for fiscal year 2025, as outlined in the Secretary's July 31, 2024 letter.
𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟒(𝐛): A comparison of obligations and expenditures with the Department's spend plan, reasons for any significant diversions, the accuracy of projections related to these diversions, and other relevant matters deemed important by the Inspector General.
𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟒(𝐜): Changes, abnormalities, or significant events related to the transfer, reallocation, or movement of funding within the Veterans Health Administration.
𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟒(𝐝): Steps the Department of Veterans Affairs can take to enhance the accuracy of supporting information submitted under 31 U.S. Code § 1105(a) and prevent future funding shortfalls.
𝐒𝐞𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝟏𝟎𝟒(𝐞): A report detailing the IG's findings and the conduct of the review to be submitted within 180 days of the Act's enactment.

𝐂𝐢𝐭𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧

The Act may be cited as the "Veterans Benefits Continuity and Accountability Supplemental Appropriations Act, 2024."
