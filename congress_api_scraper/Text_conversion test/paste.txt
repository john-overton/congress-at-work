<b>Veterans Benefits Continuity and Accountability Supplemental Appropriations Act, 2024</b>:

<b>Legislative Journey of HR 9468</b>

H.R. 9468, the <b>Veterans Benefits Continuity and Accountability Supplemental Appropriations Act, 2024</b>, commenced its legislative journey on September 6, 2024, when it was introduced in the House of Representatives. The bill was then referred to the House Committee on Appropriations.

On September 17, 2024, H.R. 9468 was the subject of debate in the House. Following forty minutes of discussion, Representative Mike Garcia moved to suspend the rules and pass the bill. The motion was agreed to by a voice vote. The House also agreed to lay the motion to reconsider on the table without objection. The bill then passed the House by a voice vote under a motion to suspend the rules.

The bill was received by the Senate on September 17, 2024. On September 19, 2024, the Senate passed the bill without amendment by a voice vote. Following the Senate's passage, a message regarding the Senate's action was sent to the House. On the same day, the bill was presented to the President, President Biden, for consideration.

On September 20, 2024, the President signed H.R. 9468 into law, officially becoming Public Law No: 118-82.

<b>Bill Contents</b>

The <b>Veterans Benefits Continuity and Accountability Supplemental Appropriations Act, 2024</b>, primarily focuses on providing supplemental appropriations for the Department of Veterans Affairs for the fiscal year ending September 30, 2024.

<b>Department of Veterans Affairs Appropriations</b>

The bill allocates:

$2,285,513,000 for "Compensation and Pensions", to remain available until expended.
$596,969,000 for "Readjustment Benefits", to remain available until expended.

<b>General Provisions</b>

The bill includes several general provisions outlining how the appropriated funds should be managed. These include:

<b>Section 101</b> states that the appropriations made available by this Act are in addition to any other amounts already appropriated for fiscal year 2024.
<b>Section 102</b> specifies that, unless otherwise stated within the Act, the additional appropriations are subject to the same authorities and conditions as the existing appropriations for fiscal year 2024.

<b>Reporting Requirements</b>

The bill mandates the submission of reports from the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to the relevant Congressional committees:

<b>Section 103(a)</b> requires a report detailing corrections the Department of Veterans Affairs will make to improve forecasting, data quality, and budget assumptions relating to budget submissions for funds under the headings "Compensation and Pensions" and "Readjustment Benefits". This report is to be submitted within 30 days of the Act's enactment.
<b>Section 103(b)</b> necessitates a report on the status of funds appropriated under "Compensation and Pensions" and "Readjustment Benefits" for fiscal years 2024, 2025, and 2026. This report is to be submitted within 60 days of enactment and updated every 90 days until September 30, 2026.

<b>Inspector General Review</b>

<b>Section 104</b> mandates an Inspector General (IG) review of the circumstances surrounding the funding shortfalls within the Veterans Benefits Administration and the Veterans Health Administration.

Specifically, the IG is to examine:

<b>Section 104(a)</b>: The circumstances surrounding and underlying causes of the announced funding shortfall in the Veterans Benefits Administration for fiscal year 2024, as detailed in the Secretary of Veterans Affairs' July 19, 2024 letter to Congress, and the announced funding shortfall in the Veterans Health Administration for fiscal year 2025, as outlined in the Secretary's July 31, 2024 letter.
<b>Section 104(b)</b>: A comparison of obligations and expenditures with the Department's spend plan, reasons for any significant diversions, the accuracy of projections related to these diversions, and other relevant matters deemed important by the Inspector General.
<b>Section 104(c)</b>: Changes, abnormalities, or significant events related to the transfer, reallocation, or movement of funding within the Veterans Health Administration.
<b>Section 104(d)</b>: Steps the Department of Veterans Affairs can take to enhance the accuracy of supporting information submitted under 31 U.S. Code ยง 1105(a) and prevent future funding shortfalls.
<b>Section 104(e)</b>: A report detailing the IG's findings and the conduct of the review to be submitted within 180 days of the Act's enactment.

<b>Citation</b>

The Act may be cited as the "Veterans Benefits Continuity and Accountability Supplemental Appropriations Act, 2024."
